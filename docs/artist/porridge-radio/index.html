Porridge Radio

Porridge Radio is an indie rock band hailing from Brighton, England, known for their raw and emotive sound. Formed in 2015, the band was initially a bedroom project by lead singer and guitarist Dana Margolin. Over time, it evolved into a four-piece group, with members including keyboardist Georgie Stott, bassist Maddie Ryall, and drummer Sam Yardley. Porridge Radio gained recognition in the British indie scene for their intense live performances and a distinctive blend of lo-fi production, existential lyricism, and emotionally charged melodies. Their breakthrough came with the critically acclaimed 2020 album "Every Bad," released under the Secretly Canadian label. The album's eclectic mix of angst-ridden anthems and introspective tracks resonated with audiences and critics alike, leading to a Mercury Prize nomination. Songs like "Sweet" and "Lilac" showcased Margolin's cathartic vocal delivery and the band's knack for balancing vulnerability with resilience. Porridge Radio's music often delves into themes of longing, identity, and emotional turmoil, capturing the complexities of modern life with both sincerity and intensity. Their contribution to the indie rock genre continues to grow, cementing their place as one of the most compelling acts of their generation.

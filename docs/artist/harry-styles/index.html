Harry Styles

Harry Styles is a British singer, songwriter, and actor born on February 1, 1994, in Redditch, Worcestershire, England. He first rose to fame as a member of the boy band One Direction, which was formed during the 2010 season of the reality TV show *The X Factor*. The group quickly became one of the best-selling boy bands of all time, known for hits like "What Makes You Beautiful" and "Story of My Life." In 2017, Styles launched a successful solo career with his self-titled debut album, which showcased a more rock-influenced sound and included the hit single "Sign of the Times." His follow-up album, *Fine Line* (2019), further solidified his status as a prominent solo artist, featuring singles like "Adore You" and "Watermelon Sugar," the latter of which won a Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance. In addition to music, Styles has pursued acting, appearing in Christopher Nolan's 2017 war film *Dunkirk* and the 2022 psychological thriller *Don't Worry Darling*. Known for his eclectic fashion sense and charismatic stage presence, Styles has become a pop culture icon in his own right, earning accolades for both his musical innovations and his ability to challenge traditional gender norms in style and performance.

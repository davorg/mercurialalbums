Jamie xx

Jamie xx, born Jamie Smith on October 28, 1988, in London, England, is a critically acclaimed music producer, DJ, and remixer known for his distinctive blend of electronic music. He gained prominence as a member of the indie pop band The xx, formed in 2005 with his school friends Romy Madley Croft and Oliver Sim. The band's debut album, "xx," released in 2009, was a major success, earning the Mercury Prize and widespread acclaim for its minimalist and emotive sound. Venturing into solo projects, Jamie xx established himself as a leading figure in the electronic music scene. His remix work, including re-imagining tracks by artists such as Adele and Florence + the Machine, showcased his talent for crafting innovative and danceable beats. In 2015, he released his debut solo album, "In Colour," which received critical praise for its vibrant soundscapes and lyrical introspection, featuring collaborations with artists like Young Thug and Popcaan. "In Colour" solidified Jamie xx as a formidable force in music production, highlighted by the track "Gosh." Throughout his career, Jamie xx has maintained a reputation for pushing musical boundaries while retaining a heartfelt, personal touch in his work.

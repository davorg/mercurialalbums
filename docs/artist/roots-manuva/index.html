Roots Manuva

Roots Manuva, born Rodney Hylton Smith on September 9, 1972, in Stockwell, London, is a pioneering figure in British hip-hop. Raised in a household rooted in Caribbean culture, his music draws from reggae, dub, and electronic influences, marking a distinctive sound that stands out in the UK music scene. He released his debut album, "Brand New Second Hand," in 1999, which gained critical acclaim for its innovative approach and lyrical depth. His breakthrough came with the 2001 album "Run Come Save Me," featuring the hit single "Witness (1 Hope)," a track that became an anthem of UK hip-hop. Roots Manuva is celebrated for his introspective and thought-provoking lyrics, often exploring themes of identity, urban life, and social issues. Throughout his career, he has collaborated with diverse artists such as Gorillaz, The Cinematic Orchestra, and Leftfield, further showcasing his versatility. His work has earned numerous accolades, including a nomination for the prestigious Mercury Prize. With a career spanning over two decades, Roots Manuva remains a vital force in music, influencing a new generation of artists with his authentic voice and genre-blending style.

Speech Debelle

Speech Debelle, born Corynne Elliot on March 17, 1983, is a British rapper, poet, and singer-songwriter known for her introspective lyrics and unique blending of hip-hop with elements of jazz and soul. Emerging from the vibrant music scene of South London, Speech Debelle made a significant impact with her debut album, "Speech Therapy," released in 2009. The album earned her critical acclaim and won the prestigious Mercury Prize, propelling her into the spotlight. Her music is often characterized by its personal and reflective nature, tackling themes of identity, resilience, and social issues. Speech Debelle's candid storytelling and distinctive voice have made her a respected figure in the music industry. Following her debut, she released subsequent albums, "Freedom of Speech" in 2012 and "Tantil Before I Breathe" in 2017, both of which continued to showcase her growth as an artist and her commitment to authentic expression. Beyond her music, Speech Debelle is known for her activism and advocacy, often using her platform to address issues such as mental health and social justice. Her work continues to inspire and resonate with listeners around the world.

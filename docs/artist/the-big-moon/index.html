The Big Moon

The Big Moon is an indie rock band hailing from London, England, formed in 2014. Known for their dynamic sound and energetic performances, the band consists of members Juliette Jackson (vocals, guitar), Soph Nathan (guitar), Celia Archer (bass), and Fern Ford (drums). Their music is characterized by catchy melodies, clever lyrics, and a blend of guitar-driven rock and pop influences. The Big Moon quickly gained attention with their debut single "Sucker" in 2015, followed by a series of successful releases that established them as a notable force in the indie music scene. In 2017, they released their debut album, "Love in the 4th Dimension," which was shortlisted for the prestigious Mercury Prize and showcased their knack for combining vibrant rock with a playful edge. Building on this momentum, The Big Moon released their sophomore album, "Walking Like We Do," in 2020. This record saw the band exploring new musical territories with more sophisticated production and expanded instrumentation. The Big Moon continues to captivate audiences with their infectious energy and heartfelt songwriting, solidifying their position as one of the UK's most exciting contemporary bands.

Jamie Woon

Jamie Woon is a British singer, songwriter, and producer known for his unique blend of soul, R&B, and electronic music. Born on March 29, 1983, in New Malden, London, Woon grew up surrounded by music, influenced by his mother, Mae McKenna, a prominent Celtic folk singer. He studied at the BRIT School for Performing Arts, which also shaped the talents of artists like Adele and Amy Winehouse. Woon first gained significant attention with his 2010 single "Night Air," co-produced by Burial, which showcased his atmospheric sound and smooth vocals. The track was featured on his debut album, "Mirrorwriting," released in 2011, which received critical acclaim for its innovative fusion of genres and introspective songwriting. After a brief hiatus, Woon returned in 2015 with his sophomore album, "Making Time," which delved deeper into a soulful, organic sound, marking a departure from the electronic influences of his earlier work. Collaborations with artists such as Royce Wood Junior and Willy Mason highlighted his versatility and commitment to artistry. Known for his emotive voice and genre-blurring compositions, Jamie Woon continues to be a respected figure within the contemporary music scene.

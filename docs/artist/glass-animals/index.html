Glass Animals

Glass Animals are a British indie pop band formed in 2010 in Oxford, England. The group is led by frontman and producer Dave Bayley, with band members Joe Seaward, Ed Irwin-Singer, and Drew MacFarlane. Known for their eclectic style, Glass Animals blend elements of indie rock, hip-hop, and electronic music to create a distinctive sound characterized by dreamy melodies, intricate beats, and vivid storytelling. Glass Animals gained significant attention with their debut album, "ZABA" (2014), which featured the hit single "Gooey" and showcased their penchant for lush, textured soundscapes. Their sophomore album, "How to Be a Human Being" (2016), continued to build their reputation, earning praise for its narrative depth and sonic diversity. The band's breakthrough moment came with their third album, "Dreamland" (2020), which includes the viral hit "Heat Waves." The song's success on streaming platforms and TikTok catapulted them to international fame and earned them a spot in major music festivals and award nominations. Glass Animals are celebrated for their energetic live performances and innovative approach to music, solidifying their status as a prominent force in the contemporary music scene.

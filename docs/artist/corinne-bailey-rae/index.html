Corinne Bailey Rae

Corinne Bailey Rae is a British singer-songwriter and guitarist, born on February 26, 1979, in Leeds, England. She rose to prominence in 2006 with her self-titled debut album, which featured the hit single "Put Your Records On." Rae's music blends elements of soul, R&B, and pop, characterized by her warm, breezy vocal style and introspective lyrics. Before her solo success, Rae was part of an indie band called Helen in the late 1990s. Her solo debut album was critically acclaimed, earning her three Grammy nominations and cementing her status as one of the UK's most promising artists. Rae followed up with her second album, "The Sea," in 2010, which was deeply personal, influenced by the sudden death of her husband in 2008. It showcased her growth as a songwriter and musician. In 2016, she released her third studio album, "The Heart Speaks in Whispers," further exploring themes of love, loss, and rebirth. Beyond her albums, Rae is known for her captivating live performances and collaborations with artists across genres. Her work continues to resonate with audiences worldwide, making her a beloved figure in contemporary music.

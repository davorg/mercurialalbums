Floating Points, Pharoah Sanders and the London Symphony Orchestra

Floating Points, the moniker of British electronic musician Sam Shepherd, has carved a unique niche in the music world with his seamless blend of electronic, jazz, classical, and world music influences. Trained as a neuroscientist, Shepherd's background in science informs his meticulous approach to music production, leading to critically acclaimed albums and energetic live performances. Pharoah Sanders, a legendary saxophonist, has been a towering figure in jazz since the 1960s, widely celebrated for his spiritual and avant-garde contributions to the genre. His collaborations with John Coltrane and distinctive overblowing techniques have left a lasting legacy, influencing generations of musicians. The London Symphony Orchestra, founded in 1904, stands among the world's most renowned orchestras, famed for its versatility and commitment to both classical and contemporary music. Its collaborations span various genres, bringing orchestral depth to diverse musical contexts. In 2021, these three forces converged on the critically acclaimed album "Promises," an extraordinary fusion that blended Shepherd's electronic sensibilities, Sanders' soulful saxophone, and the rich orchestral textures of the LSO. The project exemplifies the power of cross-genre collaboration, creating a deeply moving and meditative musical experience.

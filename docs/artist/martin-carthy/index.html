Martin Carthy

Martin Carthy is a highly influential English folk musician, born on May 21, 1941, in Hatfield, Hertfordshire, England. Known for his innovative guitar playing and distinctive vocal style, Carthy has been a seminal figure in the British folk revival since the 1960s. He first gained attention with his self-titled debut album in 1965, which showcased his intricate fingerpicking technique and ability to breathe new life into traditional British folk songs. Throughout his career, Carthy collaborated with a variety of artists and groups, including Steeleye Span, the Albion Country Band, and the folk duo with his wife, Norma Waterson, forming part of the Waterson-Carthy group. He is also recognized for his partnership with fiddler Dave Swarbrick, with whom he recorded several acclaimed albums. Carthy's adaptations of traditional songs have inspired countless musicians, including Bob Dylan and Paul Simon. With a career spanning over six decades, Martin Carthy has received numerous accolades, including multiple BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards. His dedication to preserving and transforming the folk music tradition has left an indelible mark on the genre, making him a treasured figure in the history of British folk music.

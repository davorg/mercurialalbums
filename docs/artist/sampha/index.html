Sampha

Sampha Sisay, known mononymously as Sampha, is a British singer, songwriter, and producer renowned for his soulful voice and emotionally resonant music. Born on November 16, 1988, in Morden, South London, Sampha first gained attention through his collaborations with established artists such as SBTRKT, Jessie Ware, and Drake. His ability to blend electronic elements with raw, emotive vocals quickly set him apart in the music industry. In 2013, Sampha released his debut EP, "Dual," which showcased his unique style and lyrical depth. However, it was his debut album, "Process," released in 2017, that catapulted him to greater acclaim. The album, which won the prestigious Mercury Prize, delves into themes of loss, identity, and self-discovery, highlighted by tracks like "Blood on Me" and "(No One Knows Me) Like the Piano." Sampha's music often reflects his personal experiences, marked by an introspective and heartfelt approach. His contributions to music have been lauded for their sincerity and innovative sound, positioning him as one of the standout artists in contemporary R&B and electronic music.

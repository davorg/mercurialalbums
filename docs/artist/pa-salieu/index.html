Pa Salieu

Pa Salieu, born on July 1, 1997, in Slough, England, is a British-Gambian rapper known for his unique blend of Afrobeat, dancehall, and UK rap. Raised in Coventry after moving from The Gambia at the age of ten, Salieu was exposed to diverse musical influences that shaped his distinctive sound. He rose to prominence in 2020 with the release of his breakout single "Frontline," which received widespread acclaim for its raw energy and vivid portrayal of life in Coventry. The track quickly garnered attention from major radio stations and music critics, establishing Salieu as a promising new voice in the UK music scene. In November 2020, Pa Salieu released his debut mixtape, "Send Them to Coventry," featuring collaborations with artists like M1llionz and Mahalia. The mixtape showcased his versatile style and storytelling prowess, earning him a spot on BBC Sound of 2021, which he subsequently won. Known for exploring themes of identity, struggle, and resilience, Pa Salieu continues to captivate audiences with his innovative approach to music, blending his Gambian heritage with contemporary urban sounds.

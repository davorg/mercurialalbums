The Magic Numbers

The Magic Numbers are a British indie pop band known for their harmonious melodies and distinctive blend of rock and folk influences. Formed in 2002, the band consists of two pairs of siblings: Romeo Stodart (guitar, vocals), his sister Michele Stodart (bass, vocals), Sean Gannon (drums), and his sister Angela Gannon (keyboards, percussion, vocals). They quickly gained attention for their warm, melodic sound characterized by rich vocal harmonies and close-knit instrumentation. The Magic Numbers released their self-titled debut album in 2005 to critical acclaim, with hits like "Forever Lost" and "Love Me Like You" capturing wide audiences. Their unique sound and engaging live performances helped them build a devoted fan base. Over the years, the band has continued to release music, including albums like "Those the Brokes" (2006), "The Runaway" (2010), and "Outsiders" (2018), each showcasing their evolving musical styles while retaining their signature harmonic richness. Renowned for their heartfelt songwriting and authentic live shows, The Magic Numbers remain an enduring presence in the indie music scene.

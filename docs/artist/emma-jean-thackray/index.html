Emma-Jean Thackray

Emma-Jean Thackray is a prominent British composer, producer, and multi-instrumentalist known for her vibrant blend of jazz, electronic, and experimental sounds. Originally from Yorkshire, England, she developed a deep passion for music at a young age, later honing her craft at the Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama. Thackray gained significant attention in the UK and international music scene with her eclectic approach that melds traditional jazz elements with modern influences, reminiscent of the boundary-pushing ethos of the acid jazz and fusion movements. Her debut album, "Yellow," released in 2021, showcases her ability to traverse genres, enveloping listeners in an immersive soundscape that incorporates horns, synthesizers, and rhythmic complexities. Thackray's work often transcends simple categorization, rooted in jazz yet unafraid to explore psychedelic and spiritual themes. An active performer, she frequently headlines festivals and tours, captivating audiences with high-energy live performances. Beyond her solo endeavors, she collaborates with a multitude of artists, contributing to the growing renaissance of contemporary British jazz music. Emma-Jean Thackray continually challenges musical boundaries, establishing herself as a distinguished and innovative force within the industry.

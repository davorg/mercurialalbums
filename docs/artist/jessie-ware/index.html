Jessie Ware

Jessie Ware is a British singer-songwriter known for her sophisticated blend of pop, soul, and R&B. Born on October 15, 1984, in London, England, Jessie studied English literature at the University of Sussex before pursuing a career in music. She initially gained attention as a backing vocalist for artists like Jack Peñate and featured on tracks by electronic music producers such as SBTRKT and Disclosure. Ware's debut album, "Devotion," released in 2012, was met with critical acclaim for its lush production and her sultry, expressive voice. The album earned her a nomination for the Mercury Prize and featured hits like "Wildest Moments" and "Running." Her subsequent albums, including "Tough Love" (2014), "Glasshouse" (2017), and "What's Your Pleasure?" (2020), further established her as a versatile and dynamic artist capable of evolving her sound while maintaining her signature elegance. In addition to her music, Ware is known for her engaging personality and connection with fans, exemplified by her popular podcast, "Table Manners with Jessie Ware," which she co-hosts with her mother. Combining her musical talents with charm and authenticity, Jessie Ware continues to be a significant figure in contemporary music.

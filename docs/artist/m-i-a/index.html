M.I.A.

M.I.A., born Mathangi "Maya" Arulpragasam on July 18, 1975, in Hounslow, London, is a British rapper, singer-songwriter, and record producer renowned for her eclectic musical style blending elements of hip-hop, electronic, dance, and world music. Raised in Sri Lanka during the civil war before returning to England as a refugee, her music often reflects her diverse cultural background and political convictions. She gained prominence with her debut album, "Arular" (2005), which featured the hit single "Galang." Her breakthrough came with her second album, "Kala" (2007), which included the globally acclaimed track "Paper Planes," earning her international recognition. Known for her unique approach to music and visual art, M.I.A. has been a vocal advocate for refugees and social justice, themes that frequently appear in her work. Her innovative sound and bold imagery have influenced a wide range of artists and placed her at the forefront of contemporary music. Throughout her career, M.I.A. has earned several accolades and nominations, including Grammy and Academy Award nods. Beyond music, she has ventured into filmmaking and fashion, continually pushing boundaries and challenging norms.

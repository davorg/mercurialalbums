Hannah Peel

Hannah Peel is a versatile British artist, celebrated for her achievements as a composer, producer, and musician. Born in Northern Ireland in 1985, she grew up in Yorkshire, England, where her early exposure to music sparked a lifelong passion. Peel studied at the Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts, honing her skills to work across various musical genres. She first gained attention with her experimental approach to electronic and orchestral music, often incorporating the use of unique sounds, such as music boxes, into her work. Peel's solo career took off with albums like "Awake But Always Dreaming" (2016), which was praised for its innovative exploration of electronic music and themes of memory and dementia. In addition to her solo projects, Peel has collaborated with numerous artists and has been a member of The Magnetic North, a band known for their concept-driven music. Beyond pop and electronic music, she has made significant contributions to film and television scores, showing her versatility and depth as a composer. Known for her evocative melodies and sonic curiosity, Hannah Peel continues to push the boundaries of contemporary music.

Bat for Lashes

Bat for Lashes is the stage name of Natasha Khan, a British singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist known for her eclectic sounds and ethereal aesthetic. Born on October 25, 1979, in London, Khan was raised in a multicultural family, which greatly influenced her diverse musical palette. She emerged in the mid-2000s with a unique blend of electronic, folk, and pop elements, leading to critical acclaim. Her debut album, "Fur and Gold" (2006), garnered praise for its haunting melodies and was nominated for the Mercury Prize. Khan's music often explores themes of mythology, love, and identity, drawing listeners into a dreamlike world. Her follow-up albums, including "Two Suns" (2009) and "The Haunted Man" (2012), solidified her reputation as an innovative songwriter and performer. With a penchant for visual storytelling, Khan incorporates striking visuals into her music videos and live performances, enhancing the overall experience of her art. Over the years, Bat for Lashes has carved out a distinctive niche within the music industry, celebrated for her introspective lyrics and captivating stage presence. Her continued evolution as an artist makes her a notable figure in contemporary music.

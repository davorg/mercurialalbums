Jungle

Jungle is a British modern soul musical collective founded in London in 2013 by childhood friends Josh Lloyd-Watson and Tom McFarland, known by their stage names J and T, respectively. The duo is known for blending funk, soul, electronic, and pop influences to create an infectious, groove-laden sound that resonates with audiences worldwide. Jungle first gained prominence with their self-titled debut album, released in 2014, which featured hit singles like "Busy Earnin'" and "Time." The album's success was bolstered by their visually striking music videos and dynamic live performances. Subsequent albums, including "For Ever" (2018) and "Loving in Stereo" (2021), continued to explore themes of love, life, and the modern experience, often with an emphasis on the collective's trademark euphoric and feel-good essence. Jungle's eclectic, cross-genre style is marked by lush instrumentation and a seamless fusion of vintage and contemporary sounds, attracting a diverse fan base. Their music has been featured in various media, further cementing their status as a staple in the modern music scene. As of now, Jungle remains a powerful presence, celebrated for their innovative sound and artistic vision.

Katy B

Katy B, born Kathleen Anne Brien on May 8, 1989, in Peckham, South London, is a British singer and songwriter renowned for her distinctive blend of dubstep, R&B, funky house, and UK garage music. A graduate of the BRIT School for Performing Arts and Technology, Katy B emerged on the scene as a vibrant voice in London's electronic music sphere. She first gained widespread attention in 2010 with her hit single "Katy on a Mission," produced by Benga, which showcased her ability to merge pop sensibilities with underground beats. Her debut album, *On a Mission* (2011), received critical acclaim for its dynamic sound and peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart. The album's success, driven by singles like "Lights On" and "Broken Record," established her as a leading figure in the UK music industry. She continued to explore various electronic genres with her follow-up albums, *Little Red* (2014) and *Honey* (2016), further solidifying her reputation as a versatile artist. Katy B's work is celebrated for its genuine reflection of contemporary urban life, as well as its innovative approach to blending genres, making her a distinctive voice in modern British music.

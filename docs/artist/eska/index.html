Eska

Eska Mtungwazi, known professionally as Eska, is a Zimbabwean-British singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist renowned for her unique fusion of jazz, folk, soul, and electronica. Born in 1971 in the United Kingdom to Zimbabwean parents, Eska grew up immersed in a rich tapestry of musical influences. She studied at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama, nurturing her talent and laying a foundation for her eclectic style. Eska's career began to gain momentum in the early 2000s with her collaboration on influential projects, including work with British electronic duo Zero 7 and The Cinematic Orchestra. Her expansive vocal range and emotive delivery captivated audiences and distinguished her as a sought-after collaborator. In 2015, Eska released her self-titled debut album, which was met with critical acclaim, earning her a nomination for the prestigious Mercury Prize. The album showcased her exceptional skill in blending diverse genres while maintaining a deeply personal and evocative lyrical narrative. Eska's work continues to resonate widely, celebrated for both its technical mastery and its capacity to transcend traditional musical boundaries. Her artistry is defined by a fearless exploration of sound and a profound connection to her cultural roots.

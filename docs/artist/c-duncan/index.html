C Duncan

C Duncan, born Christopher Duncan on July 29, 1989, in Glasgow, Scotland, is a Scottish musician and composer known for his distinctive blend of classical and contemporary sounds. Raised in a musically inclined family, he was exposed to a rich tapestry of influences from a young age. His mother was a viola player and his father a composer, which helped nurture his musical talents. Duncan studied at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland, where he honed his skills in classical composition. In 2015, Duncan released his debut album "Architect," which garnered critical acclaim and was nominated for the prestigious Mercury Prize. His music is characterized by lush, multi-layered harmonies and intricate arrangements, often drawing comparisons to artists like Fleet Foxes and Sufjan Stevens. Duncan followed up with "The Midnight Sun" in 2016, further establishing his reputation for crafting atmospheric and introspective soundscapes. His third album, "Health," released in 2019, marked a shift towards a more polished pop sound, drawing on personal experiences and collaborations. Duncan's work is celebrated for its melodic richness and emotional depth, securing his place as a distinctive voice in modern music.

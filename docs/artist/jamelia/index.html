Jamelia

Jamelia Niela Davis, known professionally as Jamelia, is a British R&B and pop singer-songwriter, television presenter, and actress born on January 11, 1981, in Birmingham, England. She rose to prominence in the early 2000s with her distinctive voice and a string of successful hits. Her debut album, "Drama," released in 2000, brought her initial recognition, but it was her sophomore album, "Thank You," released in 2003, that catapulted her to broader fame. The album featured hit singles like "Superstar," "Thank You," and "See It in a Boy's Eyes," which won her several awards and established her as a significant figure in the UK music scene. Jamelia's sound blends contemporary R&B with pop influences, often featuring poignant lyrics. Her talent has earned her multiple MOBO Awards and BRIT Award nominations. Beyond music, Jamelia has pursued various television roles, appearing in shows as a panellist and presenter, including ITV's "Loose Women." Her acting credits also include roles in film and television dramas. Known for her advocacy on social issues, Jamelia continues to be a strong voice in the entertainment industry, balancing her public persona with personal commitments as a mother of two.

Faithless

Faithless is an iconic British electronic music act formed in 1995, known for their unique blend of house, trip-hop, and dance music. The group was founded by producer and DJ Rollo Armstrong, sister and vocalist Dido, rapper and vocalist Maxi Jazz, and multi-instrumentalist and producer Sister Bliss. They gained widespread acclaim with their debut album, "Reverence" (1996), which included hit singles like "Insomnia" and "Salva Mea," both of which became anthems in the club scene. Over the years, Faithless released several successful albums, including "Sunday 8PM" (1998), "Outrospective" (2001), and "No Roots" (2004). Their music is characterized by its profound lyrical content, often exploring themes of spirituality, love, and existential contemplation, paired with infectious beats and melodies. Renowned for their powerful live performances, Faithless earned a formidable reputation on the international festival circuit. Despite Maxi Jazz's departure from the group in 2011, Faithless continued to produce music, with Sister Bliss and Rollo at the helm. In 2020, they released "All Blessed," their first studio album in a decade, reaffirming their enduring impact on the electronic music landscape.

Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds

Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds is the solo project of English musician Noel Gallagher, former lead guitarist and chief songwriter of the iconic rock band Oasis. Formed in 2010 after Oasis disbanded in 2009, Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds presents a departure from the brash Britpop sound of Oasis, exploring a blend of rock, alternative, and psychedelic influences. Noel released the self-titled debut album in 2011, which was met with commercial success and critical acclaim, featuring hits like "The Death of You and Me" and "Everybody's on the Run." The band's lineup has varied over the years but has consistently featured high-profile musicians who support Gallagher's creative vision on tours and recordings. Known for his sharp wit and influential songwriting, Noel infuses his work with reflective lyrics and expansive instrumentation. Subsequent albums like "Chasing Yesterday" (2015) and "Who Built the Moon?" (2017) further showcase Gallagher's evolving musical style, incorporating elements of jazz, electronic, and experimental sounds. Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds continue to captivate audiences worldwide with their dynamic live performances and innovative studio releases, securing Noel Gallagher's place in modern rock music.
